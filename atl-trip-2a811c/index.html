★ MY PICK

Option 1 — the lowest-risk all-rounder

It hits every box we named: a real soaking tub for Valerie's baths, full kitchen, and — uniquely — it's single-story and step-free, which keeps stairs out of the picture for a curious 5-year-old. It's the only one with a proven track record (15 reviews, Guest Favorite, professionally managed), and a guest literally wrote "very safe neighborhood that we walked nightly." It's mid-priced. I'd book the refundable rate (~$662) for ~$48 of peace of mind. Pick Option 2 only if being closest to Robb + the pool seals it; pick Option 3 if saving ~$130 outweighs the thin reviews and the railing question. Option 4 (the Birch Nest) is the most-reviewed of the bunch and a Superhost — but it has no bathtub and sits furthest from Robb, so it's the call only if baths stop being a must-have.

🛡️ Neighborhood safety · independent data, not just Airbnb

Outside crime data for the three towns. Exact addresses aren't shown until booking, so this is town-level — safety still varies block to block.

🏙️Kennesaw — Options 1 & 3

Overall crime ~32% below the U.S. average; safety grade B. Violent crime ~34% below average. The one elevated category is burglary (~21% above average) — typical of a suburb. Widely described as a welcoming, family-friendly town.

🌳Acworth — Option 2

Even safer overall — about 16 incidents per 1,000 residents vs ~33 nationally; safer than ~80% of Georgia cities and ~68% of U.S. cities. Violent crime ~70% below the national rate. Property crime is its relatively higher category but still below the U.S. per-capita average.

🏞️Cartersville — Option 4 (Bartow Co.)

Safety score 71/100; overall crime ~15% below national. Violent crime is very low (about a third of the national rate), but property crime runs slightly above the national average — which pairs awkwardly with Option 4's missing CO alarm and shared, unfenced yard.

Bottom line: all three towns are solidly safe family areas — Acworth scores highest, Cartersville is fine but a touch more property-crime-prone. Across the board the relatively higher category is property crime / burglary (normal for car-dependent suburbs), so the exterior cameras on every listing are a genuine plus; just lock up and don't leave valuables visible in the car. Once we have the booked address, a 2-minute check on the local crime map for that exact street is worth it.
Sources: AreaVibes, Niche, BestPlaces, DoorProfit, NeighborhoodScout, CrimeGrade, CrimeSafe (2026 figures).

🔎 Blindspots worth a thought

Things easy to miss when all four look nice in photos.

📍None are "in Atlanta"

All four are NW suburbs — Options 1–3 are ~25–30 mi from downtown (35–45 min); Option 4 (Cartersville) is further, ~40 mi. Perfect for a shower-centered weekend; budget drive time if you want the Aquarium or downtown sights.

🚨Option 4's trade-offs

The Birch Nest is the most-reviewed, but it has no bathtub (shower only — a miss for Valerie), no carbon-monoxide alarm, no in-unit laundry, and it's the upstairs unit above a separate (empty) apartment with a shared, not-fully-fenced backyard.

🔄Refund windows differ a lot

Kids get sick. Opt 2 cancels free until the day before; Opt 3 locks Jul 3; Opt 1's cheap rate is non-refundable. Flexibility has real value here.

🪜Option 3's railing flag

Airbnb's own listing flags "heights without rails or protection" on Option 3 — could be a loft, open stairwell, or unrailed deck. Worth asking the host about with Valerie around. Option 1 is single-story and step-free.

🏊A pool is a supervision job

With a 5-year-old, an unfenced community pool means eyes-on every second — and on a brand-new listing you don't know the hours, rules, or upkeep.

🆕New-listing risk (Opt 2)

Zero reviews + first-time host = trusting the photos, and a small chance the Friday-evening lockbox check-in hiccups with a tired kid in tow.

🏡It's a lived-in home (Opt 3)

Garage + office locked off, drawers may hold the owner's stuff, only 1 review on cleanliness. Charm vs. uncertainty.

🕐Check-in & the shower schedule

All three are self/lockbox check-in (Opt 1 is pro-managed). Friday arrival is fine — just confirm the timing lines up with Saturday's shower so no one's scrambling.
