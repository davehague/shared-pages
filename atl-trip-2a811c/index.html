🛡️ Neighborhood safety · independent data, not just the listings

Town-level crime data (exact addresses come after booking). All five sit in solidly safe family areas — here's the nuance.

🏞️Kennesaw — Option ①

Solidly safe — roughly 32% below the national rate (around a B grade), a classic family suburb anchored by Kennesaw State and the national battlefield park. Property crime is the higher category, as in any suburb.

🌳Woodstock — Option ②

One of metro Atlanta's most popular family towns (Cherokee County) — consistently rated very safe with low violent crime and a thriving, walkable downtown. The kind of place people move to for the schools and Main Street.

🏡East Cobb / Marietta — Options ③, ⑤ + the shower

City-wide Marietta reads above national, but that's driven by retail-corridor theft — the residential East Cobb neighborhoods (incl. 30062, where the shower is) earn high family-safety grades. The specific area you'll be in is the safe part.

🌲Acworth — Option ④ (near Rob)

Safest of the NW towns — ~16 incidents per 1,000 vs ~33 nationally, safer than ~80% of GA cities; violent crime ~70% below the national rate.

Bottom line: none of these are safety concerns. The common thread is property crime (normal for car-dependent metro suburbs) — lock up and don't leave valuables in the car. Once you have the booked street address, a 2-minute crime-map check for that block is worth it.
Sources: NeighborhoodScout, AreaVibes, Niche, BestPlaces, CrimeGrade (2026 figures).

🍴 Eat, drink & Saturday markets

A starter set near the shower & your candidate bases (East Cobb / Kennesaw / Woodstock). Once you lock a base I'll build this out properly for that exact neighborhood — for now, the can't-miss is the Saturday-morning market before the 1pm shower.

🧺 Saturday farmers markets — the morning plan

  • Marietta Square Farmers Market — Sat 9am–noon, Mill St lot. Closest to the shower side; pair with the historic square's shops.
  • Woodstock Farmers Market — Sat mornings on the green; right by Main St if you base in Woodstock (②).
  • Roswell Farmers Market — Sat 8:30am–noon (38 Hill St); a pretty alternative ~15–20 min east.

🏘️ Cute towns to wander

  • Marietta Square — classic town square ringed with shops & restaurants, right on the shower side.
  • Downtown Woodstock — walkable Main St, amphitheater, breweries & cafes (Option ② is steps away).
  • Downtown Kennesaw — small historic strip ~1 mi from Option ①; trains, shops, casual eats.

🍦 Coffee & sweets — Val's pick

  • Downtown Woodstock coffee + dessert — several local cafes & sweet shops along Main St.
  • Marietta Square ice cream / treats — easy stop after the market.
  • Kennesaw coffee roasters — near Option ① if you base there.

🍔 Friday near the aquarium
