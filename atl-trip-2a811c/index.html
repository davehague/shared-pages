Option 5 — the Corner House (Acworth)

With Option 1 gone, this is the new best all-rounder. It checks the everyday boxes: full kitchen for breakfast, soaking tubs in both bathrooms, a fully fenced backyard (genuinely nice with a 5-year-old), and in-unit laundry. It's proven (4.8 ★ from 44 reviews) and in Acworth — close to Robb. The one real catch: it's a split-level — about 5 steps up to the door, then ~7 more up to the bedrooms — so it's not stair-free. Close runners-up: Option 2 (the pool + closest to Robb + most flexible cancellation, but unproven) and Option 4 (the Birch Nest — most reviews + Superhost + single-level, but no tub and furthest out). Now that baths are optional, Option 4 is back in real contention.

🛡️ Neighborhood safety · independent data, not just Airbnb

Outside crime data for the three towns. Exact addresses aren't shown until booking, so this is town-level — safety still varies block to block.

🌳Acworth — Options 2, 5 & 6

The safest of the three — about 16 incidents per 1,000 residents vs ~33 nationally; safer than ~80% of Georgia cities and ~68% of U.S. cities. Violent crime ~70% below the national rate. Property crime is its relatively higher category but still below the U.S. per-capita average. Three of our five are here.

🏙️Kennesaw — Option 3

Overall crime ~32% below the U.S. average; safety grade B. Violent crime ~34% below average. The one elevated category is burglary (~21% above average) — typical of a suburb. Welcoming, family-friendly town.

🏞️Cartersville — Option 4 (Bartow Co.)

Safety score 71/100; overall crime ~15% below national. Violent crime is very low (about a third of the national rate), but property crime runs slightly above the national average — which pairs awkwardly with Option 4's missing CO alarm and shared, unfenced yard.

Bottom line: all three towns are solidly safe family areas — Acworth scores highest (and is where most of our options sit). Across the board the relatively higher category is property crime / burglary (normal for car-dependent suburbs), so the exterior cameras on every listing are a genuine plus; just lock up and don't leave valuables visible in the car. Once we have the booked address, a 2-minute check on the local crime map for that exact street is worth it.
Sources: AreaVibes, Niche, BestPlaces, DoorProfit, NeighborhoodScout, CrimeGrade, CrimeSafe (2026 figures).

🔎 Blindspots worth a thought

Things easy to miss when all five look nice in photos.

🍳Option 6 has no kitchen + one bath

The Hummingbird is a gorgeous historic suite — but only a fridge/microwave (no real kitchen to cook breakfast) and one bathroom for all four of us, including Saturday-morning getting-ready for the shower. Charm, but awkward logistics for this trip.

🪜Option 5 is split-level

The Corner House isn't stair-free: ~5 steps up to the front door, then ~7 more up to all the bedrooms and baths. Manageable, but worth picturing with Valerie and Grandpa. Upside: a fully fenced backyard, which none of the others have.

📍None are "in Atlanta"

All five are NW suburbs — Options 2/3/5/6 are ~25–30 mi from downtown (35–45 min); Option 4 (Cartersville) is further, ~40 mi. Great for a shower-centered weekend; budget drive time for downtown sights.

🔄Refund windows differ a lot

Kids get sick. Opt 2 cancels free until Jul 16, Opt 6 until Jul 12, Opt 3 locks Jul 3, Opt 4 is standard, and Opt 5's cheaper rate is non-refundable (pay ~$68 more to make it refundable).

🚨Option 4: no CO alarm + furthest

The Birch Nest is the most-reviewed and single-level, but it has no carbon-monoxide alarm (host says not required), no in-unit laundry, a shared unfenced yard, and it's the furthest drive from Robb.

🏚️Option 6: charming but old

An 1879 Victorian is "charmingly creaky" (the host provides a white-noise machine), and a guest noted the shower-inside-the-antique-clawfoot-tub feels small/unsafe — fine for bathing little Valerie, awkward for adults.

🆕Option 2 is unproven

Zero reviews + first-time host = trusting the photos, and a small chance the Friday-evening lockbox check-in hiccups with a tired kid in tow. The pool is also a constant-supervision job for a 5-year-old.

🏡Option 3 is a lived-in home

Garage + office locked off, drawers may hold the owner's stuff, only 1 review on cleanliness, plus the "heights without rails" flag. Cheapest and roomiest, but the least-known quantity.
