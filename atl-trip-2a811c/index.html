🍴 Eat & drink near home base

Local gems, casual, kid-friendly — nothing fancy or late, no reservations needed. Most are a short hop along the Roswell Rd corridor near the Sandy Springs house. (Double-check hours before you go.)

⚡ Quick / counter / takeaway

  • Just Love Coffee — order at the counter, savory eats to go.

🍔 Friday near the aquarium

🛡️ Neighborhood safety · independent data, not just Airbnb

Town-level crime data (exact addresses come after booking). All are solidly safe family areas — here's the nuance.

🌆Sandy Springs — Option 7

Very safe — overall crime ~16.5 vs ~33 nationally, with very low violent crime (1.44). Safer than most communities its size. The most central base, and a strong safety profile.

🏡East Cobb / Marietta — Option 8 + the shower

City-wide Marietta reads ~43% above national, but that's driven by retail-corridor theft — the residential East Cobb neighborhoods (incl. 30062, where the shower is) earn high family safety grades. The specific area you'll be in is the safe part.

🌳Acworth — Options 2, 5 & 6

Safest of the NW towns — ~16 incidents per 1,000 vs ~33 nationally, safer than ~80% of GA cities; violent crime ~70% below the national rate.

🏞️Kennesaw (Opt 3) & Cartersville (Opt 4)

Both solidly safe — Kennesaw ~32% below national (grade B); Cartersville score 71/100 with very low violent crime. Property/burglary is the relatively higher category in each, as in any suburb.

Bottom line: none of these are safety concerns. The common thread is property crime (normal for car-dependent metro suburbs), so the exterior cameras on every listing are a plus — lock up and don't leave valuables in the car. Once you have the booked street address, a 2-minute crime-map check for that block is worth it.
Sources: NeighborhoodScout, AreaVibes, Niche, DoorProfit, BestPlaces, CrimeGrade (2026 figures).

🔎 Blindspots worth a thought

Things easy to miss when they all look nice in photos.

🧭Central beats near-Robb on driving

The shower's in East Cobb and you fly in/out of ATL, so a central base (Sandy Springs / Marietta) saves ~1.5 hrs of total driving across the weekend vs an Acworth base — with shorter hops for a tired Val. Acworth's only real edge is being closest to Robb's actual house.

🪜Stairs are the recurring catch

Most of these aren't stair-free: Option 7 has no inside-stair handrails (per a review), Option 5 is split-level, Option 6 is a 2nd-floor suite, Option 3 has the railing flag. Only Option 4 is truly single-level. Worth picturing with Val + Grandpa Bob.

🍳Option 6 has no kitchen + one bath

Gorgeous historic suite, but only a fridge/microwave (no cooking breakfast) and one bathroom for all four — awkward for Saturday-morning getting-ready. More a couples' getaway than a family base.

🆕Two are basically unproven

Option 2 (first-time host, 0 reviews) and Option 8 (1 review) are gambles on the photos. Free/standard cancellation softens it, but with a tired kid arriving Friday, a proven host (7, 4, 6) is one less thing to worry about.

🔄Refund windows differ

Free-cancel: Opt 2 & 7 to Jul 16, Opt 6 to Jul 12, Opt 3 to Jul 3. Opt 5's cheaper rate is non-refundable (pay ~$68 more to make it refundable). Kids get sick — flexibility has value.

🎟️Friday aquarium = book ahead

Pre-booked tickets are cheaper than same-day, and it's the one outing that fits the schedule. With an 11:35 landing you've got time; keep it to ~2 hrs of highlights so Val isn't fried before Saturday.
