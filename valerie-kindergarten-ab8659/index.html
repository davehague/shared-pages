Readiness, the non-academic kind

⏳

Practice waiting

The principal's #1 Kindergarten-readiness skill. Take turns as a family — brushing teeth, getting in the car, picking the movie. Modeling patience builds the self-control school asks for all day.

🧺

Give one weekly job

A small, real responsibility builds confidence. For Valerie's age: get the mail, set the table, pick up toys when done, or get her own snack.

How the first days work

⏰

Daily rhythm

8:00 AM — doors open
8:15 AM — classes begin / late bell
2:50 PM — dismissal

🚗

Car drop-off

Use door #15 (Hennessey side) or door #2 (Whitney side). Kids exit passenger side only, parents stay in the car, follow the path — don't park in drop-off lanes or staff lots.

🔄

First-week teacher cycling

Kindergartners rotate through every K teacher the first week so staff can build balanced classes. You'll hear from the whole K team that week, then get assigned to one teacher after the first full week.
